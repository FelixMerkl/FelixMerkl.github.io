Felix Merkl | Vessel Visualizations (Work in Progress)

State of the art vessel visualizations

Illustrative visualization methods try to improve spatial relations and depth and shape perception of an object by encoding differnet perception cues into outstanding features in shading options or additional augmentations.

Try the visualization methods in a WebGL application here.

Basic Shaded Model

A normal shaded object with specular highlights and solid color.

wire frame model

The vessel rendered as wire frame model, i.e. only edges of the model are shown. In this case, the edges are the connection of the model's vertices.

Fresnel

The transparency of the object is dependent on the normal vectors and the camera perspective.

Frosted

The transparency of the object is dependent on the depth.

Chromadepth

The depth is encoded as color.

Outline

Shows only the silhouette of the model.

Hatching

A computer generated hatching of an objects. Resembles a hand drawn object.

Shadow Projection

Two walls as auxiliary augmentations are used as projection planes for a virtual shadow of the object.
