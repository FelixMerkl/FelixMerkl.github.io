Learn About Me

Currently, I am working at the TUM (Technische Universität München) as research assistant. In my Master's Thesis, I created new visualization techniques for medical augmented reality applications. They focus on improving depth and shape perception for medical doctors during a minimal invasive surgery by enhacing features of the vessel. For evaluation, the techniques are compared to state-of-the-art visualization techniques.


A small prestudy indicated promising effects and therefore, I have the opportunity to keep working at the project as research assistant. The goal is to elaborate the study design, conduct an in-depth study, and publish the results in a scientific paper. Until then, I unfortunately cannot show any results on this page.


The resulting system has five different visualizations implemented: three state-of-the-art methods and two own created. The system is used during a laprascopic surgery. It takes the camera image, tracking data from the patient and instrument, and creates an augmented reality with a real-world surgery image and correctly placed virtual vessels. These vessels are rendered with a visualization method that the surgeons can select. To simulate an applicable laprascopic surgery, focus and context are used to blend the real world with the augmentations.

ACCUSTOMED VISUALIZATION TECHNIQUES

In minimal-invasive surgery, it is crucial for the surgeons to be aware of hidden structures. Blood vessels, for example, can be covered by tissue, organs, and fat. Therefore, augmented reality is used to visualize the hidden blood vessels and project them onto the real world image.


Visualization techniques are used to further improve shape and depth perception. Most methods are illustrative and add perception cues in the form of color, transparency, and feature enhancement. Blending augmentations with the real world

The key in mixing virtual objects into the real world is occlusion. It is a very strong cue which gives medical doctors spacial awareness. This can also be misleading, when virtual objects are placed on top of the real world when in reality it is the other way round. Therefore, focus and context methods are used to show the order of objects with additional occlusion cues.


Learn more about the functionality and implementation of the most common techniques.

pq edge effect

The most important features are extracted and placed on top of the background and augmented object to add occlusion.
Window effect

Parts of the background are removed to create a see-through effect onto augmented objects.
